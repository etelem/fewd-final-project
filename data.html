Show your boss data on employee engagement...

It's important to emphasize that workplace culture isn't just about making your office more fun - it's about creating a great culture by engaging your employees; engagement will, over time, drastically improve organizational performance. Don't just use anecdotal evidence - show your boss the data below from a study by the Harvard Business Review in partnership with The Energy Project.

The below exhibits data from the Quality of Life at Work Study - by the Energy Project in partnership with the Harvard Business Review. They surveyed 20,000 employees around the world using a 56 question survey.

According to the Life at Work findings - there are 4 core needs at work:

  1. Physical - to rest and renew
  2. Emotional - to feel valued
  3. Mental - to be empowered to set boundaries and focus in an absorbed way
  4. Spiritual - to find a sense of meaning and purpose in your work

Only 7% of people have all 4 core needs met at work!

This chart shows the ratio of needs met and unmet. Time for creative or strategic thinking and ability to focus on one thing at a time are severly lacking (72% and 68% unmet respectively).

46% of people surveyed are dissatisfied with their jobs (to only 37% satisfied). As shown, satisfied employees are 125% more engaged! Satisfied employees will put forth much greater effort in their work - which can make a drastic difference for a company's bottom line!

Showing the importance of meeting employee core needs. The results are staggering - ~200% increase in employee loyalty for those with all 4 needs met!
